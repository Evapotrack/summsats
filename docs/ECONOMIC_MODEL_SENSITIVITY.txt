================================================================================
SUMMSATS — ECONOMIC MODEL SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS
Stress-testing the 750x margin under varying conditions
Creator: Andrew Brown
Date: April 6, 2026
================================================================================

PURPOSE

The SummSats concept document establishes a 750x margin between what the user
pays per entry (1,500 sats) and what the AI costs to process it (~2 sats).
This document stress-tests that margin under varying Bitcoin prices, API
pricing changes, usage patterns, and operational scenarios to determine how
robust the economic model actually is.


================================================================================
BASELINE MODEL
================================================================================

    Entry cost to user:     1,500 sats (fixed, not configurable)
    AI cost per entry:      ~2 sats (Claude Haiku, ~8,000 input tokens)
    Margin:                 ~750x

    Bitcoin price assumed:  $60,000 / BTC (1 sat = $0.0006)
    Haiku pricing assumed:  $1.00 per million input tokens,
                            $5.00 per million output tokens
    (Source: Anthropic pricing page, confirmed April 2026)

    Per-entry AI cost breakdown:
        Input: ~8,000 tokens × $1.00/1M = $0.0080
        Output: ~750 tokens × $5.00/1M = $0.00375
        Total: ~$0.0118 per entry
        In sats: $0.0118 / $0.0006 = ~19.6 sats

    NOTE: The concept document estimates 1-3 sats per entry. Using current
    Haiku pricing ($1/$5 per million tokens) and realistic token counts,
    the actual cost is closer to ~20 sats per entry. The margin is ~75x,
    not 750x. The 750x figure in the concept document appears to use
    older or estimated pricing. This analysis uses the more conservative
    75x figure.

    Corrected baseline:
        Revenue per entry:  1,500 sats
        AI cost per entry:  ~20 sats
        Margin:             ~75x
        This is still highly favorable.


================================================================================
SENSITIVITY TO BITCOIN PRICE
================================================================================

The user pays in sats. The developer pays the API bill in USD. The margin
depends on the sat-to-USD exchange rate.

    BTC Price    1 sat =      AI cost/entry    Sats/entry cost    Margin
    -----------------------------------------------------------------------
    $20,000      $0.0002      $0.0118          59 sats            ~25x
    $40,000      $0.0004      $0.0118          30 sats            ~50x
    $60,000      $0.0006      $0.0118          20 sats            ~75x
    $100,000     $0.0010      $0.0118          12 sats            ~125x
    $200,000     $0.0020      $0.0118          6 sats             ~250x
    $500,000     $0.0050      $0.0118          2 sats             ~750x


ANALYSIS: The margin improves as Bitcoin price rises (each sat covers more
USD cost). Even at $20,000 per BTC — a significant price decline from
current levels — the margin is 25x. The model breaks even (margin = 1x)
only if Bitcoin drops below approximately $787 per BTC, which would
represent a 99% decline from current levels.

CONCLUSION: The model is extremely robust against Bitcoin price declines.
It remains profitable at any Bitcoin price above ~$800.


================================================================================
SENSITIVITY TO API PRICING
================================================================================

Anthropic may change Haiku pricing. The model needs to withstand significant
price increases.

    Haiku Price          AI cost/entry    Sats/entry cost    Margin
    Multiplier           (USD)            (at $60K BTC)      
    -----------------------------------------------------------------------
    1x (current)         $0.0118          20 sats            ~75x
    2x                   $0.0236          39 sats            ~38x
    5x                   $0.0590          98 sats            ~15x
    10x                  $0.1180          197 sats           ~8x
    20x                  $0.2360          393 sats           ~4x
    50x                  $0.5900          983 sats           ~1.5x
    75x                  $0.8850          1,475 sats         ~1.0x

ANALYSIS: At 10x current Haiku pricing, the margin is still 8x — comfortably
profitable. At 75x current pricing, the margin is essentially break-even.
The model breaks even at approximately 75x current Haiku pricing.

A 10x price increase is plausible if Anthropic significantly reprices Haiku
or if the user must switch to a larger model. A 50x increase would require
using Claude Opus (the most expensive model) — which is unnecessary for this
task.

CONCLUSION: The model withstands up to approximately 75x API price increase
before breaking even. Even a switch from Haiku to Sonnet (roughly 4-5x more
expensive) keeps the margin above 10x.


================================================================================
SENSITIVITY TO USAGE PATTERNS
================================================================================

The per-entry economics are fixed. But total revenue and total cost scale
with usage.

    Usage Pattern         Entries/yr    User pays      AI costs      Net
                                        (sats/yr)      (sats/yr)     (sats)
    -----------------------------------------------------------------------
    Light (1/week)        52            78,000         1,040         76,960
    Moderate (3/week)     156           234,000        3,120         230,880
    Heavy (1/day)         365           547,500        7,300         540,200
    Intensive (3/day)     1,095         1,642,500      21,900        1,620,600

    All figures at $60,000 BTC, current Haiku pricing ($1/$5 per MTok).

ANALYSIS: Usage pattern does not affect the margin (it is always ~75x at
current pricing). Higher usage means more absolute sats collected but also
more API cost. The margin percentage is constant.

The wallet send threshold of 25,000 sats is reached at:
    Light use:     ~4 months (17 entries)
    Moderate use:  ~6 weeks (17 entries)
    Heavy use:     ~17 days (17 entries)
    Intensive use: ~6 days (17 entries)


================================================================================
SENSITIVITY TO TOKEN COUNT
================================================================================

The baseline assumes ~8,000 input tokens per AI call. This could vary based
on entry length and summary complexity.

    Avg tokens/call    AI cost/entry    Sats/entry    Margin
                       (USD)            (at $60K)
    -----------------------------------------------------------------------
    4,000              $0.0063          10 sats        ~150x
    8,000              $0.0118          20 sats        ~75x
    16,000             $0.0228          38 sats        ~39x
    32,000             $0.0448          75 sats        ~20x
    64,000             $0.0888          148 sats       ~10x
    100,000            $0.1375          229 sats       ~7x

ANALYSIS: Even if the average input doubles to 16,000 tokens (which would
require very long entries), the margin is 39x. At 100,000 tokens (the
extreme case of a massive entry that pushes context limits), the margin is
still 7x.

The design caps effective input by sending only the summary + last 10
entries. This means token count is bounded regardless of total entry count.
The worst case is 10 very long entries + a 500-word summary, which might
reach 30,000-50,000 tokens.

CONCLUSION: Token count variability does not threaten the model.


================================================================================
WORST-CASE SCENARIO
================================================================================

Combining the most pessimistic plausible assumptions simultaneously:

    Bitcoin price:      $20,000 (67% decline from $60,000)
    API pricing:        10x current (Anthropic dramatically reprices)
    Token count:        32,000 per call (very long entries consistently)

    AI cost per entry:  ~$0.0448 × 10 = $0.448
    Sat value:          $0.0002
    Sats per entry:     $0.448 / $0.0002 = 2,240 sats

    At 2,240 sats cost vs 1,500 sats revenue: THE MODEL LOSES MONEY.

    But this scenario requires THREE simultaneous extreme events. If only
    TWO of the three occur:

    $20K BTC + 10x API (normal tokens):
        AI cost: ~$0.118 / $0.0002 = 590 sats. Margin: ~2.5x. Thin but viable.

    $20K BTC + 32K tokens (normal pricing):
        AI cost: ~$0.0448 / $0.0002 = 224 sats. Margin: ~6.7x. Comfortable.

    10x API + 32K tokens ($60K BTC):
        AI cost: ~$0.448 / $0.0006 = 747 sats. Margin: ~2x. Thin but viable.

CONCLUSION: The model fails only under a simultaneous triple-shock scenario
that is extremely unlikely. Under any combination of two adverse factors,
the model remains viable (thin but positive in all two-factor cases).


================================================================================
KEY ECONOMIC INSIGHT
================================================================================

The structural advantage of SummSats' model: revenue is direct (user
explicitly pays 1,500 sats per entry) and AI cost is a rounding error
(~20 sats per entry, ~75x corrected margin). The key lesson: charge for the
user-valued action, not for the AI processing.


================================================================================
ON-CHAIN COST ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Each entry payment is a Bitcoin transaction that carries an on-chain fee.
This fee is paid by the SENDER (the user, from their external wallet), not
by the app. But it affects the total cost to the user.

    Fee rate       Tx vsize     Fee paid        Total cost to user
    (sat/vB)       (est.)       (sats)          (sats)
    -----------------------------------------------------------------------
    1              141          141             1,641
    2              141          282             1,782
    5              141          705             2,205
    10             141          1,410           2,910
    20             141          2,820           4,320
    50             141          7,050           8,550

ANALYSIS: At low fee rates (1-5 sat/vB), the on-chain fee adds 9-47% to
the entry cost. At high fee rates (20-50 sat/vB), the on-chain fee exceeds
the entry cost itself. Users will naturally avoid writing entries during
high-fee periods, creating a natural throttle.

MITIGATION: The app does not control when the user sends payments. But the
lock screen could display current fee estimates (from mempool.space) so the
user can decide whether to wait for lower fees. This is not in the current
spec but would be a low-effort quality-of-life addition.

FUTURE: Lightning payments (deferred feature) would reduce per-entry payment
cost to near-zero fees, making the full 1,500 sats the only cost.


================================================================================
BREAK-EVEN SUMMARY
================================================================================

The model breaks even (AI cost = revenue) when:

    1,500 sats × sat_value_usd = AI_cost_usd
    1,500 × (btc_price / 100,000,000) = AI_cost_usd

    At current Haiku pricing (~$0.0118/entry):
        Break-even BTC price = $0.0118 × 100,000,000 / 1,500 = $787

    At 10x Haiku pricing (~$0.118/entry):
        Break-even BTC price = $0.118 × 100,000,000 / 1,500 = $7,867

    At current BTC price ($60,000), Haiku price must reach:
        $60,000 × 1,500 / 100,000,000 = $0.90 per entry
        Current cost is $0.0118, so pricing must increase ~76x

CONCLUSION: The SummSats economic model is robust. It survives extreme
Bitcoin price declines, significant API price increases, and high token
variability. Only a simultaneous triple-shock scenario threatens it. The
75x corrected margin at current conditions provides enormous headroom.
